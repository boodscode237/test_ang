Challenge 3: The Digital Detective Story

🕵️ A Day in the Digital City – Read carefully!

Maya is a 14‑year‑old who dreams of becoming a software developer. One afternoon, she was testing a function she wrote for a game – a small piece of code that made her character jump(). But something was wrong: the character jumped through walls! That was a bug, specifically a logic error. Maya started debugging and found that she forgot to add a parameter that checks for walls.

Later, her friend Leo sent her a link to “free coins” for another game. Maya remembered her cybersecurity lessons: it could be a phishing attack or even a Trojan hiding inside. She also knew that a firewall and antivirus are essential defenses. She blocked the message.

At school, they learned about Artificial Intelligence. A neural network uses layers of neurons to recognize patterns. For example, a chatbot like Siri uses machine learning to understand voice commands. Maya’s teacher explained that algorithms are like recipes, and training data helps AI make predictions.

On her phone, Maya installed a new mobile app from the App Store. She checked the permissions – why would a calculator need her location? She denied it. Later she bought a game using a digital wallet and got a receipt by email. She also wrote a review (5 stars) for the game.

Finally, Maya helped her cousin create a strong password and turn on 2‑step login for social media. She explained that encryption scrambles data so hackers can’t read it. Maya felt like a true tech detective. She even used a tracking number to follow an online order for new headphones. The future is bright with AI, but we must stay cyber‑smart!
