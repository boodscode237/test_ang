Challenge 3: The Great Digital Adventure

📘 Read the story carefully:

Lena is a 13-year-old student who loves technology. Every day after school, she uses her laptop to explore the digital world. Her laptop has a fast SSD, so it starts up in seconds. For her school project, she needs to create a presentation about "How the Internet Works."

First, Lena opens her browser, Chrome. She types the URL www.kidzexplore.com into the address bar and presses Enter. Her computer breaks this request into tiny data packets. A router in her house directs these packets through the internet—a giant network of networks—to a powerful server. The server finds the website and sends the data packets back. Lena's laptop receives them and puts them back together to display the webpage. The whole process takes only a few seconds!

The website has a fun game about databases. Lena learns that a database is like a digital filing cabinet. In the game, she organizes a table for a school library. Each row in the table is a record for one book (Title, Author, Year). Each column is a field, like "Author Name." She also learns about a Unique ID—a special number that is different for every book, so you can tell them apart even if they have the same title.

Later, Lena wants to share her project with her friend, Sam. She decides to upload it to the cloud using her Google Drive. She knows the cloud isn't actually in the sky; it's just data stored on a big server in a data center somewhere in the world. She attaches her file, which is 5 megabytes (MB), and clicks "Send."

After her homework is done, Lena wants to relax. She opens her favorite online game. But when she logs in, she gets a strange message from a user named "GameMaster99." The message says, "Click this link for free game money!" The link has a strange URL. Lena remembers the lessons from her cybersecurity class. She knows about trick messages and fake profiles. She doesn't click the link. Instead, she closes the message and reports the user.

Later, she video calls Sam to tell him about the message. Sam says he got a similar one. They agree it's important to have strong passwords and never share them. They also talk about how cyberbullying can happen and why it's important to save the proof and tell a trusted adult. Lena feels good knowing she is a smart and safe digital citizen.
